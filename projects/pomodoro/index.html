Pomodoro Technique

po·mo·do·ro
    /ˌpäməˈdôrō/ (adjective):
  1. A variety of pear-shaped tomato from Italy.
  2. A time management method that uses a timer to break down work into intervals, separated by short breaks. These intervals are named pomodoros, after the tomato-shaped kitchen timer that Francesco Cirillo used as a university student.
